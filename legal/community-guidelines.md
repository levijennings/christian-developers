# Community Guidelines

**Last Updated:** April 2026

## Welcome to Christian Developers

Christian Developers is a community for Christian professionals in technology to connect, grow, share resources, and support one another in integrating faith and work. These Community Guidelines establish the standards for creating a welcoming, respectful, and productive community environment.

## Our Community Values

**Faith and Integrity:** We operate from Christian principles of honesty, integrity, and treating others as we wish to be treated.

**Respect and Inclusion:** We welcome Christians from all denominational backgrounds and traditions. Respect for theological diversity within Christianity is essential.

**Professional Excellence:** We maintain professional standards in our interactions and expect quality contributions.

**Mutual Support:** We encourage members to support one another in career development, spiritual growth, and navigating faith in the workplace.

**Thoughtful Dialogue:** We engage in substantive discussion while respecting differing perspectives.

## 1. Faith-Inclusive Environment Expectations

### 1.1 What We Welcome

- Members of all Christian traditions (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant, Pentecostal, Methodist, Reformed, Baptist, etc.)
- Respectful discussion of theological topics and denominational differences
- Sharing how faith informs your work and professional decisions
- Questions about integrating faith and career
- Prayer requests and spiritual support
- Mentorship that considers faith dimensions of career
- Community events centered on Christian faith and fellowship

### 1.2 Denominational Diversity

The Christian Developers community intentionally includes Christians from diverse traditions. To maintain respect:

**Do:**
- Acknowledge that different Christian traditions have valid theological perspectives
- Explain your tradition respectfully without dismissing others
- Engage with curiosity about perspectives different from your own
- Find common ground in core Christian beliefs
- Respectfully debate theology when appropriate to the conversation

**Don't:**
- Declare other Christian denominations as "non-Christian" or "heretical" in dismissive ways
- Suggest members of other traditions won't go to heaven or lack genuine faith
- Require agreement on peripheral doctrines as a condition of community membership
- Dismiss entire denominations as theologically inferior
- Use dehumanizing language about Christians from other traditions
- Create sectarian divisions within the community

### 1.3 Examples of Respectful Theological Discussion

**Respectful:** "In my Reformed tradition, we understand predestination this way... I know Armenians approach this differently. How does that work in your tradition?"

**Not Respectful:** "Your denomination's view on predestination is heretical. Real Christians believe..."

**Respectful:** "I'm Catholic and believe in the importance of church tradition alongside Scripture. I know many Protestants prioritize sola scriptura. How do you approach this?"

**Not Respectful:** "Protestants ignore church wisdom. Catholics actually understand the faith."

**Respectful:** "Our church practices infant baptism, while your tradition practices believer's baptism. Both approaches have thoughtful theological reasoning behind them."

**Not Respectful:** "Your baptism practices are unbiblical. Only our approach is scripturally valid."

## 2. Respectful Discourse Policy

### 2.1 Standards for All Discussions

**Be Constructive:**
- Focus on ideas, not personalities
- Assume good faith in other members' contributions
- Offer solutions, not just criticism
- Build on others' contributions when possible

**Listen to Understand:**
- Read carefully before responding
- Ask clarifying questions if confused
- Don't mischaracterize others' positions
- Acknowledge valid points even in disagreement

**Disagree Respectfully:**
- "I see that differently because..." rather than "You're wrong because..."
- "I respectfully disagree with this perspective..." instead of attacking
- Focus on substantive differences, not personal qualities
- Recognize that disagreement doesn't mean disrespect

**Maintain Boundaries:**
- Keep discussions on-topic
- Don't escalate conflicts
- Accept when others decline to continue a discussion
- Respect moderator guidance

### 2.2 What Respectful Discourse Looks Like

**Professional Disagreement:**
- "I have a different perspective on this approach..."
- "That's interesting. Have you considered...?"
- "I respectfully disagree because..."
- "Can you help me understand your reasoning on this point?"

**Addressing Tone Issues:**
- "I think we might be misunderstanding each other. Let me clarify..."
- "I don't think that's what they meant. Let's ask for clarification..."
- "That came across more harshly than intended. What did you mean?"

**Acknowledging Limitations:**
- "I'm not an expert in this area, but..."
- "Different people have different experiences with this..."
- "That's outside my knowledge, but here's what I've heard..."

### 2.3 When Discussions Go Sideways

If a discussion becomes heated:
- Pause and take time before responding
- Re-read what you wrote from the other person's perspective
- Ask yourself if your response serves the community
- Consider whether the thread should continue or be closed

If you see a discussion becoming unproductive:
- Flag it for moderation using the report feature
- Don't pour fuel on the fire with escalating comments
- Consider private messaging someone if you have concerns

If you're involved in a conflict:
- Try to resolve it privately with the other party
- Involve a moderator if you can't resolve it
- Accept moderator guidance on resolution
- Move forward gracefully

## 3. Content Standards for Forum Posts

### 3.1 Topic Appropriateness

**Appropriate Topics:**
- Technical challenges and solutions
- Career development and job opportunities
- Integrating faith and work
- Industry news and trends
- Mentorship and professional relationships
- Workplace challenges from a Christian perspective
- Community announcements and events
- Prayer requests and spiritual support

**Borderline Topics (Allowed with Care):**
- Politics insofar as it relates to Christian perspectives on work
- Social issues from a faith perspective
- Theology and denominational topics (if respectful and relevant)
- Controversial workplace issues from a Christian ethics perspective

**Off-Topic Content (Not Allowed):**
- Political campaigns, candidates, or partisan promotion
- Non-Christian spiritual practices or religions
- Unrelated commercial promotion or MLM schemes
- Technical topics unrelated to Christian developers
- Sports, entertainment, hobbies (unless related to networking)
- Dating and romantic advice (unless workplace-related)

### 3.2 Post Quality Standards

Posts should:
- Be clearly written and easy to understand
- Include sufficient detail for others to engage meaningfully
- Be on-topic and relevant to the community
- Not be duplicate posts of the same content
- Include relevant context for technical questions
- Link to resources rather than copying large blocks of text

### 3.3 Formatting and Professionalism

- Use clear formatting with paragraphs and line breaks
- Avoid ALL CAPS (seen as shouting)
- Use appropriate language and avoid profanity
- Spell-check and proofread when possible
- Keep a professional tone while being friendly
- Use emojis sparingly and appropriately

## 4. Prayer Requests Standards

### 4.1 What Prayer Requests Are For

Prayer requests allow members to share personal needs with the community for prayer and support. Prayer requests can be:
- Personal challenges or struggles
- Family matters
- Health concerns
- Career transitions or difficulties
- Spiritual growth concerns
- Loss and grief
- Celebrations and thanksgivings

### 4.2 Confidentiality Expectations

**When Posting:**
- Consider what information is comfortable sharing publicly
- Mark requests as "private" if you want limited visibility
- Remember that community members will see your request
- Understand that members may follow up with you

**When Responding:**
- Respect the confidentiality of what's shared
- Don't share prayer requests outside the community
- Don't offer unsolicited advice or judgment
- Respond with compassion and support
- Respect if someone declines responses

### 4.3 Community Response Standards

When responding to prayer requests:
- Offer genuine, compassionate support
- Keep responses brief and respectful
- Avoid advice unless specifically requested
- Don't proselytize or push your theology
- Respect the requester's approach to their situation
- Follow up if you said you would

## 5. Mentorship Relationship Boundaries

### 5.1 Professional Relationships

Mentorship through Christian Developers should be:
- **Professional:** Focused on career development and professional growth
- **Appropriate:** Maintaining clear professional boundaries
- **Consensual:** Both parties agreeing to the relationship and time commitment
- **Respectful:** Of each person's time, expertise, and personal boundaries
- **Ethical:** Without conflicts of interest or inappropriate power dynamics

### 5.2 What Appropriate Mentorship Looks Like

- Regular meetings (frequency agreed upon)
- Focused on career development, skills, and professional challenges
- Discussion of work experiences and lessons learned
- Support through career transitions
- Introduction to professional networks when helpful
- Discussion of faith integration in work
- Respect for boundaries and time constraints

### 5.3 Boundary Violations

The following are NOT appropriate in mentorship relationships:
- Romantic or sexual advances
- Inappropriate personal conversations
- Requests for personal loans or financial arrangements
- Pressure to do favors outside mentorship scope
- Sharing of confidential business information
- Using the relationship for personal business gain
- Abuse of power or authority
- Violating privacy or confidentiality

### 5.4 What to Do If Boundaries Are Violated

If boundaries are violated:
- Communicate clearly that the behavior is not appropriate
- Terminate the mentorship relationship
- Report the violation to levi@dvlmnt.com with details
- We will investigate and take appropriate action
- Your confidentiality will be respected

## 6. Reporting and Moderation Process

### 6.1 How to Report a Violation

**Use In-App Reporting:**
- Click the report button on specific posts or profiles
- Describe the issue
- Provide context if helpful

**Email Reports:**
- Send to levi@dvlmnt.com with "Community Violation Report" in subject
- Include description, context, and reference to the violating content
- Provide your contact information

**Reporting Serious Issues:**
- For harassment, threats, or illegal activity, report immediately
- Provide as much detail as possible
- We will prioritize serious reports

### 6.2 What Happens When You Report

- We will review your report promptly
- We may reach out for additional information
- We will take appropriate action (ranging from warning to suspension)
- We will keep you informed of the outcome (when appropriate)
- Retaliation against reporters is prohibited and will be dealt with seriously

### 6.3 Moderation Decisions

**We May:**
- Remove posts that violate guidelines
- Edit posts for formatting or to remove specific inappropriate content
- Issue warnings to members about violations
- Suspend members temporarily for repeated violations
- Terminate accounts for serious violations
- Report illegal activity to appropriate authorities

**We Will:**
- Enforce guidelines consistently
- Explain decisions when removing content
- Provide appeal opportunities
- Keep records of violations
- Work with members to improve behavior when possible

**We Won't:**
- Censor legitimate disagreement or diverse perspectives
- Punish theological discussion conducted respectfully
- Remove posts simply because others disagree
- Play favorites based on member status
- Tolerate harassment or discrimination

### 6.4 Appeals

If you believe a moderation decision was unfair:
- You can appeal within 30 days
- Email levi@dvlmnt.com with "Moderation Appeal" in subject
- Explain why you believe the decision was incorrect
- Provide context or evidence supporting your position
- We will review and respond within 14 days

## 7. Member Conduct and Accountability

### 7.1 Mutual Accountability

The Christian Developers community works best when members help maintain standards:

**You Can:**
- Gently address issues privately if you feel comfortable
- Report violations using the appropriate channels
- Model respectful community engagement
- Welcome new members and help them understand norms
- Encourage others to follow guidelines

**You Shouldn't:**
- Take on enforcement roles you don't have
- Call out violations publicly in a shaming way
- Assume malice in others' words
- Hold grudges against members who violate guidelines

### 7.2 Progressive Discipline

For violations, we typically follow this approach:
1. First Violation: Warning and explanation of the guideline
2. Second Violation: Temporary content removal and repeated warning
3. Third Violation: Short-term suspension (1-7 days)
4. Fourth+ Violations: Extended suspension or termination

Serious violations may skip steps based on severity.

### 7.3 Second Chances

We believe in redemption and growth. Members who violate guidelines can:
- Learn from mistakes
- Improve their behavior
- Be welcomed back into the community
- Make amends if appropriate

We do not permanently hold violations against members who demonstrate change.

## 8. Creating a Welcoming Community

### 8.1 Welcome New Members

- Introduce yourself and offer to help
- Answer questions about community norms
- Suggest relevant forums or discussion threads
- Be patient with newcomers who are learning norms
- Help them find mentors or connect with others

### 8.2 Celebrate Diverse Perspectives

- Value different Christian traditions represented
- Learn from perspectives different than your own
- Ask genuine questions about others' experiences
- Acknowledge valid points even in disagreement
- Find common ground across differences

### 8.3 Support Members

- Offer genuine encouragement and support
- Celebrate others' successes and growth
- Provide help when you have expertise
- Listen without always offering solutions
- Recognize the vulnerability in sharing struggles

## 9. Enforcement and Accountability

DVLMNT is committed to enforcing these guidelines fairly and consistently. Violations can result in:

- **Warnings:** For first-time minor violations
- **Content Removal:** For inappropriate posts
- **Temporary Suspension:** For repeated violations
- **Permanent Termination:** For serious violations or threats

## 10. Questions About Guidelines

If you have questions about these guidelines or whether something is appropriate:
- Email levi@dvlmnt.com
- Ask in the "Community Help" forum thread
- Contact a moderator privately

## 11. Final Thoughts

Christian Developers is a gift to the community. Together, we maintain an environment where:
- Christian developers can be authentic about their faith
- People from different traditions respect and learn from one another
- Professional growth and spiritual growth are integrated
- Members support and encourage one another
- Faith and work are meaningfully connected

Thank you for being part of creating this community.
